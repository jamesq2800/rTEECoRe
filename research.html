Research

Our Research Portfolio

rTEECoRe supports a range of multi-institutional studies focused on the use of transesophageal echocardiography in emergency and critical care settings.

Core Infrastructure

Resuscitative TEE Registry

The rTEECoRe registry is the foundation of our research network — a standardized, multi-institutional database capturing clinical data on the use of TEE in acute care settings across participating sites.

The registry enables investigators to pool data across institutions, dramatically increasing statistical power and the ability to generate meaningful, practice-changing evidence. Standardized data elements ensure comparability across sites and over time.

Cardiac Arrest Research

Cardiac Arrest Research

Cardiac arrest remains one of the most time-critical medical emergencies, with survival heavily dependent on the quality and speed of resuscitation. rTEECoRe supports several studies examining how TEE can improve cardiac arrest management and outcomes.

In-Hospital Emergencies

In-Hospital Emergencies

Beyond out-of-hospital cardiac arrest, TEE has a growing role in the management of acute in-hospital emergencies. rTEECoRe investigates TEE use in rapid response scenarios, peri-operative crises, and other acute deteriorations occurring within the hospital setting.

Critical Care

Microcirculation in Shock

Shock states are characterized not only by macrovascular failure but by profound dysfunction at the microvascular level. This research program examines how echocardiographic imaging can shed light on microcirculatory dysfunction in critically ill patients.

[Additional description to be added]

Recovery

RECOVER Program

Survival from cardiac arrest is only the beginning. The RECOVER Program follows survivors longitudinally to characterize recovery trajectories — physical, cognitive, and psychological — and identify modifiable factors that influence outcomes after resuscitation.
