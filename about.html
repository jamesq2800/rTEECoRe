About rTEECoRe

Who We Are

A multi-institutional collaborative research network uniting emergency medicine, intensive care, cardiology, and anesthesiology leaders to advance TEE research in acute care.

Vision

Our Vision

To accelerate the development of outcome-oriented research and knowledge translation on the use of transesophageal echocardiography in emergency and critical care settings.

Mission

Our Mission

rTEECoRe pursues four strategic goals to build a strong, evidence-based foundation for resuscitative TEE in acute care:

01

Foster Partnerships

Build clinical partnerships among emergency departments and intensive care units using TEE across institutions.

02

Standardize Data Collection

Enable multi-institutional data sharing through harmonized data definitions and reporting standards.

03

Establish Research Infrastructure

Build and maintain shared systems for data capture, management, and analysis across participating sites worldwide.

04

Share Data Openly

Maximize benefits for clinicians and researchers by making collaborative datasets openly accessible to qualified investigators.

Background

Our Background

Formed in 2019 as an initiative of the Resuscitative TEE Project, rTEECoRe unites leaders from emergency medicine, intensive care, cardiology, and anesthesiology with a shared commitment to advancing the science of resuscitative TEE.

While studies have demonstrated the feasibility and safety of focused TEE in acute settings, most published research remains limited to small, single-center observations. These studies lack the statistical power to generate meaningful evidence on patient outcomes. rTEECoRe was created specifically to address this gap — by enabling larger, multi-institutional research that can produce the rigorous evidence clinicians need to guide practice.

rTEECoRe continues to grow its network of participating institutions and expand its research portfolio.

Organizational Structure

How We're Organized

rTEECoRe operates as a collaborative research network with shared governance across participating institutions. Leadership is drawn from faculty across member sites with shared coordinating infrastructure.

The network is structured to enable both centralized coordination and distributed participation — individual sites contribute data, expertise, and investigators while sharing in the governance and scientific direction of the network.

Participating Institutions

Our Network

rTEECoRe brings together academic medical centers and health systems committed to advancing resuscitative TEE research. Participating institutions contribute clinical data, expertise, and investigators to our shared research mission.

Interested in joining the rTEECoRe network? We welcome institutions that perform resuscitative TEE in emergency or critical care settings and are committed to standardized data collection.
